# maimai Community Ecosystem

## Community Content

- **majdata / MajdataPlay**: An open-source maimai chart simulator/editor. The "majdata" format is widely used by fan charters. MajdataPlay (`LingFeng-bbben/MajdataPlay` on GitHub) lets players play custom charts on PC.
- **AstroDX** (formerly MaiChartManager): A popular mobile maimai simulator available on iOS/Android, allowing players to play fan-made charts. It has its own charting community.
- **MaiChartManager**: A tool for managing and converting maimai chart files, often used alongside simulators.
- **Score tracking**: Players use **Diving-Fish's Maimaidx Prober** (`www.diving-fish.com/maimaidx/prober/`) — a widely-used score tracker and rating calculator for maimai DX, especially popular in the Chinese community. There is also **maimai.sega.jp** for official JP score tracking (Aime linked).
- **YouTube / Bilibili**: Extensive AP/SSS+ gameplay videos, chart previews, and hand-cam content.

## Wikis

- **maimai wiki (Fandom)**: `maimai.fandom.com` — English-language wiki covering songs, charts, game mechanics, and version history.
- **Gamerch maimai Wiki**: `gamerch.com/maimai` — A Japanese wiki with detailed song lists, chart constants, and technical data.
- **maimai DX info (Japanese)**: Various Japanese fan sites track internal chart level values (constants) used for rating calculation.
- **Diving-Fish** also serves as a de facto reference for chart constants and difficulty data in the Chinese community.

## Forums and Community Hubs

- **Reddit**: `r/maimai` — English-speaking community hub for discussion, score sharing, and arcade location info.
- **Discord**: Multiple servers exist, including a main English-language maimai Discord and regional servers (SEA maimai, etc.). AstroDX also has its own Discord.
- **Twitter/X**: Active JP community under hashtags like `#maimai` and `#maimaiでらっくす`. Many players post scores and AP achievements.
- **Bilibili** (`bilibili.com`): The primary Chinese community platform — hosts gameplay videos, tutorials, and chart showcases. Many top players post here.
- **Weibo / QQ Groups**: Chinese players also organize via QQ groups and Weibo for local arcade meetups and score sharing.
- **NicoNico**: Some Japanese players post gameplay content here alongside YouTube.

## Regional Note

The Chinese and Japanese communities are significantly larger than the English-speaking one, reflecting the game's arcade availability in those regions and Southeast Asia.
