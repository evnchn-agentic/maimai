# What is a Rhythm Game?

A rhythm game is a genre of video game where gameplay revolves around performing actions in time with music or a rhythmic pattern. The core challenge is matching player input to visual or audio cues with precise timing.

## Key Characteristics

- Gameplay is fundamentally tied to music or beat patterns.
- Players are scored based on timing accuracy (e.g., "Perfect," "Great," "Miss").
- Difficulty typically scales through faster tempos, more complex note patterns, and additional input lanes.
- Strong emphasis on repetition and skill improvement.

## Gameplay Mechanics

- Notes or prompts scroll toward a judgment line; the player must act when they align.
- Combo systems reward consecutive successful inputs, often with score multipliers.
- Health or life bars may deplete on missed notes, potentially causing a fail state.
- Songs are "charted" — each difficulty level has a hand-authored map of notes synced to the music.

## Common Input Methods

- **Controller/keyboard:** Button presses mapped to lanes (e.g., DDR's arrow pad, Guitar Hero's fret buttons).
- **Touch screen:** Tapping, holding, and swiping on mobile devices (e.g., Cytus, Arcaea).
- **Specialized peripherals:** Dance pads, plastic guitars, turntable controllers, and taiko drums.
- **Motion/full body:** Camera or sensor-based tracking (e.g., Just Dance).

## Notable Examples

- **PaRappa the Rapper** (1996) — a pioneering title in the genre.
- **Dance Dance Revolution** (1998) — popularized rhythm games in arcades worldwide.
- **Guitar Hero** (2005) and **Rock Band** (2007) — brought the genre to mainstream console audiences with instrument peripherals.
- **osu!** (2007) — a free-to-play, community-driven PC rhythm game with multiple modes.
- **Beat Saber** (2019) — a VR rhythm game where players slash blocks with virtual sabers.
- **Arcaea**, **Phigros**, **Cytus** — notable mobile rhythm games with innovative chart mechanics.
