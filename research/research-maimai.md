# What is maimai?

maimai is an arcade rhythm game developed and published by SEGA, first released in Japan on July 11, 2012. It is distinguished by its unique circular "washing machine"-shaped cabinet and gameplay interface.

## Gameplay Mechanics

The game features a round screen surrounded by 8 touch-sensitive buttons arranged at equal intervals around the perimeter (like a clock). Notes fly outward from the center toward the edges, creating a 360-degree play field.

### Input Types

- **Tap**: Hit the corresponding button when a note reaches the edge.
- **Hold**: Press and sustain a button for the duration of the note.
- **Slide**: Drag along the touch-sensitive screen surface following a path.
- **Touch**: Tap directly on the touchscreen (introduced in DX versions).
- **Break**: A special high-value tap note with distinct scoring weight.

The circular design allows note patterns to approach from any direction, setting maimai apart from most rhythm games that use a linear or lane-based layout.

## Scoring System

Each note is judged on timing accuracy with grades:

- Critical Perfect
- Perfect
- Great
- Good
- Miss

The final score is calculated as a percentage (up to 101% when all Break notes receive Critical Perfect bonuses). Players receive letter rankings (SSS, SS, S, A, B, etc.) based on their achievement percentage.

## Versions and Iterations

### Original Series

1. **maimai** (2012) — the original release.
2. **maimai PLUS** (2012)
3. **maimai GreeN / GreeN PLUS** (2013)
4. **maimai ORANGE / ORANGE PLUS** (2014)
5. **maimai PiNK / PiNK PLUS** (2015–2016)
6. **maimai MURASAKi / MURASAKi PLUS** (2016–2017)
7. **maimai MiLK / MiLK PLUS** (2017–2018)
8. **maimai FiNALE** (2019) — the final version of the original series.

### DX Series (Reboot)

- **maimai DX** (2019) — major reboot with a new cabinet featuring a larger screen and Touch notes.
- Subsequent versions: DX PLUS, Splash, Splash PLUS, UNiVERSE, UNiVERSE PLUS, FESTiVAL, FESTiVAL PLUS, BUDDiES, BUDDiES PLUS.

## Popularity

maimai has become one of the most popular arcade rhythm games in Japan and has expanded significantly across East and Southeast Asia (notably China, South Korea, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Singapore, and Thailand). The franchise boasts a massive song library spanning J-pop, anime, Vocaloid, game music, and original compositions. Its accessible yet deep gameplay loop appeals to both casual players and hardcore rhythm game enthusiasts.
